# CYA 2 Reflection

## Activity description: what were the expectations and what did you do?

The expectation was to select an activity that pushes my professional development and produces a verifiable deliverable. I chose to independently learn Quantum Computing, specifically Grover's Algorithm. I spent approximately 12 hours reading foundational texts (like the book Nielsen's Quantum Computation and Quantum Information), setting up a local quantum simulation environment using IBM's Qiskit, writing code to build quantum circuits, and documenting my findings in a comprehensive technical report applying the algorithm to Sudoku and SAT problems.

## Technical or professional decisions: what key decisions did you make? Why?

Strategically, I chose to use IBM's Qiskit rather than other frameworks because it has the most robust open-source community and excellent documentation, which is crucial for self-directed learning. Technically, I decided to test the algorithm on a $2 \times 2$ Sudoku and a SAT problem rather than just basic unstructured search. I made this decision to prove that I could translate abstract quantum theoretical concepts into practical constraint-satisfaction problems, bridging the gap between physics and computer science. I also chose to draft my foundational report in Chinese to solidify my deep understanding of the complex math (like amplitude amplification) before applying the code in a universal engineering context.

## Contributions: did you work independently or with others?

I worked completely independently on this project. As a self-directed learner, I took full responsibility for setting up the Conda environment, writing the Python code, debugging quantum circuit matrix errors, and generating the final analytical report. I did, however, leverage open-source documentation and tutorials from the Qiskit community as my learning baseline.

## Quality assessment: How do you assess your performance? What would you improve?

I assess my performance as highly proficient. I successfully achieved the primary goal: writing working quantum circuits that simulate faster-than-classical search. However, if I were to repeat this experience, I would improve it by deploying the code onto real quantum hardware via IBM Quantum Lab, rather than just using the qasm_simulator. Furthermore, I would attempt to scale the Sudoku model beyond $2 \times 2$, analyzing how the noise in current quantum hardware affects the accuracy of the amplitude amplification.

## Career alignment: How did this experience move you closer to your career goals?

This experience was highly aligned with my goal of becoming a top-tier software engineer. It demonstrated my ability to quickly learn a completely new, mathematically heavy programming paradigm. The tech industry values engineers who are not afraid of steep learning curves. By successfully teaching myself the basics of quantum algorithms and producing a tangible code repository, I have added a unique, cutting-edge talking point to my resume that differentiates me from candidates who only possess traditional classical computing skills.
